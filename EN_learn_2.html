Keyword optimization strategy

Last time we discussed the importance of App Store Optimization for your app.
 
Let’s quickly recall the three pros of regular ASO activities:

  1. App Store search provides a large number of users: according to Apple, 65% of users download the app they’ve discovered via search.
  2. Users coming from search queries are much more loyal than those from paid ads. According to Appsflyer, customers acquired through search have a 150% higher retention rate than users acquired through paid commercials.
  3. App Store optimization takes about several hours of work before version release and costs almost nothing at the same time driving relevant customers to your app.
 
Good news is that ASO is not rocket science, everyone can perform it after having learnt the processes of collecting and processing data, as well as identifying and implementing the steps based on the collected info.
 
First things first, let’s define the main goal of ASO. As we see it, the main goal is to maximize the search traffic for your app. By search traffic, we mean relevant users alone, nothing else.
 
There are three stages of text search optimization we adopted within ASOdesk.
 

Stage 0: Analyzing the market to decide whether ASO is vital or not

 
Previously we mentioned brand queries for app subcategories. For instance, if your app is yet another messenger, you should lean heavily on other channels apart from ASO. But if you are developing a dating app, ASO can be of great help as the quantity of general search queries will be high in this case.
 


So on this stage, we recommend getting the full understanding of the potential amount of search traffic in your app subcategory. Thus you’ll be able to set KPI and have proper expectations.
 

Stage 1: Keyword analysis

 
Keyword analysis is the process of creating a semantic core, a set of words and phrases that correspond with your app and your customers’ needs most of all. Imagine what words would your customer use to describe his problem or urge and the solution to it, what search query he would use. Try to find as many combinations as possible, this will be your app’s semantic core.
 
There are three sources of inspiration to create this set of words. First, there is your common sense: you are familiar with your app better than anyone, you’ll easily come up with 10-15 basic search queries.Then there are suggestions and related searches in the App Store — this is what a user sees while typing in his query.

And the last, but not least — competitive intelligence: some of your rivals most likely have already performed ASO, all you have to do is find these keywords.
 
Now that you’ve created the semantic core with several hundred queries you have to sort them out.
When you are customizing your app metadata for the App Store, you’ve got only 160 characters at your disposal: 30 characters for the title, 30 characters for the subtitle, 100 characters for the keywords. You can’t use queries from outside the App Store: web search and App Store algorithms are completely different. App Store can provide a very limited amount of worthful queries.
 
 

How to choose keywords:

  • Choose only those keywords which show more than zero estimated queries a day (you can check this, for example, in ASOdesk — see the dashboard screenshot). Don’t waste away the precious characters on some useless query that will drive no customers. Forget about web search queries, they have nothing to do with App Store and won’t do you good.
  • Mind the relevance of the query, it has to be very strong. An app only gets to top in search results when users download it after finding it by this very query, or in other words, those who satisfy this particular need of the customer. 
 
 

How to understand if a query is relevant?

It’s easy: look at the apps that are already in the top by this search query (see the ASOdesk Keywords Analytics screenshot example). If these apps are competing with yours, then — out of the blue! — the query is relevant.
 

 

Stage 2: Cycles

In the very beginning, you can’t predict what position your app will reach after optimization in results to a certain query and how many users there are with the same query. That’s why you should tune the app metadata over and over again. It means ASO activities should be performed on a regular basis. Before the version release spare some time to analyze your current situation and renew the semantic core — and soon you’ll reap what you sow!
