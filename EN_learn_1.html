Keywords Optimization in ASO. Why Is It Important?

You’ve created your first (second, tenth) iOS app? Congrats! By the way, have you ever wondered how many apps there are in the App Store besides yours? According to the latest stats — 2.2 million. And many developers seem to ignore this fact and keep paying little or no regard to search optimization. Fools, they have no perception! (c)
 
App store optimization allows customising your app meta-data in order to acquire as many relevant customers as possible.
 
But let’s start ab ovo: Cost per Loyal User grows by 12,2% year-over-year. Every year it becomes more and more difficult for an ordinary developer to acquire users for the app:
 

 

 

What’s so great about users who discover an app through the search?

 
  1. Search offers the largest amount of users. According to Apple, 65% of users download the app they’ve discovered via search. Have a look at the App Store bottom bar: 20% of it is occupied by search icon, which makes it one of the most essential sources of customer acquisition.
  2. Users coming from search queries are much more loyal than those who come through paid ads. According to Appsflyer, customers acquired through search have a 150% higher retention rate than users acquired through paid commercials.
  3. App Store optimization takes about several hours of work before version release and costs almost nothing at the same time driving relevant customers to your app.
 

Are you saying that search is the main traffic source for my app since it provides 65% of users?

 
Not quite: it all depends on the type of your app. Suppose you released a new messenger app — chances are you’ll have to struggle for search traffic as most users will just look for certain apps by their names, like Facebook or Viber. In such categories, a large share of traffic is driven by brand queries. On the contrary, if you have a news app or a music app, or a dating app, there is much potential for gaining search traffic, because there’ll mostly be general queries. 
 
Here’s the ASOdesk index diagram in which we categorized apps into “subcategories” different from native App Store categories: for example, there’s no such category as “Taxi” in App Store.
 

 
On the diagram, you can see the share of brand queries (dark blue) in the total amount of traffic in each subcategory. In the case of taxi apps, these will be “lyft”, “uber” while general queries are “taxi”, “taxi app”, etc. Also, you can see the top-20 apps that people search for using brand queries and top-20 search queries in US App Store based on ASOdesk data.
 
 

Lesson Summary

  1. App Store search provides a large amount of relevant traffic, so ASO is a must have for any app developer or marketer.
  2. The potential for search traffic depends on the type of your app: some may turn out inconsistent, so you should be ready to make it up by other channels.
  3. Search provides almost no cost traffic, as the amount of time required for setting up the ASO meta-data (when done on a regular basis) is nothing.
